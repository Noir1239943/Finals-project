Exploring the rich coffee culture of the Philippines offers a unique blend of tradition, sustainability, and community. In regions like the Cordilleras, particularly in Benguet, coffee farms such as Agnep Heritage Farms cultivate shade-grown Arabica beans at elevations over 1,600 meters above sea level.

These farms employ sustainable practices, including organic farming and community engagement, to produce award-winning coffee while preserving the environment .

For those interested in experiencing the diverse flavors of Philippine coffee, establishments like El Union Café in La Union and Commune in Makati serve as bridges between consumers and local coffee producers.

These cafés source their beans directly from farmers in regions like Benguet and Bukidnon, offering patrons a taste of the country's rich coffee heritage .
